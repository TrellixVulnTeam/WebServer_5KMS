My email (the same day):

Hannah! Outstanding!

Yes, for the second interview with the same interviewers, keep digging deeper into your pile of questions. Here are 3 pointers.

First, make sure to use expressions such as….

“I’m so glad I have another chance to speak with you because something you said last time really stuck with me. You mentioned [insert whatever here]. I would love it if you could elaborate on that especially as it relates to…[insert whatever here].”

This builds a deeper relationship with the interviewer and shows you are genuinely interested in his or her insight.

Second, make sure to pick out new questions you didn’t get to ask the first time so they can see you are extensively prepared.

This demonstrates you are well researched and interested.

Third, mention to the interviewer something like…

“When I was here last time, I spoke with [insert name here] and she mentioned [insert whatever here]. I would LOVE to get your viewpoint and thoughts on that…

This third one will help you ensure consistency across people in the company. It will create an engaging dialogue and foster camaraderie. It makes the interviewers feel as though you are an inclusive p erson and team player.
